SUPPLIER ONBOARDING & INTEGRATION GUIDE
========================================
Version: 3.0 | Effective: January 2025

WELCOME TO SUPPLIERSYNC
------------------------
This guide will help you understand the SupplierSync platform, integration 
requirements, and best practices for maintaining your product catalog and pricing.

SYSTEM OVERVIEW
---------------
SupplierSync is an AI-powered platform that:
- Automates catalog management
- Optimizes pricing strategies
- Enforces governance policies
- Provides real-time analytics
- Enhances customer experience

PLATFORM AGENTS
---------------
The system uses specialized AI agents:

1. SUPPLIER AGENT
   - Monitors supplier catalogs
   - Updates product availability
   - Manages wholesale pricing
   - Handles inventory changes
   - Updates product descriptions

2. BUYER/PRICING AGENT
   - Optimizes retail pricing
   - Analyzes market conditions
   - Adjusts for competitiveness
   - Implements pricing strategies
   - Considers margin requirements

3. CX (CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE) AGENT
   - Monitors customer feedback
   - Analyzes reviews and ratings
   - Identifies product issues
   - Tracks customer sentiment
   - Recommends improvements

4. ORCHESTRATOR
   - Coordinates all agents
   - Enforces business rules
   - Applies governance policies
   - Tracks metrics and costs
   - Manages data consistency

INTEGRATION REQUIREMENTS
------------------------
To integrate with SupplierSync, you must provide:

1. Product Data:
   - SKU (unique identifier)
   - Product name and description
   - Category classification
   - Wholesale pricing
   - Current availability status

2. Pricing Data:
   - Wholesale cost per unit
   - Recommended retail price (optional)
   - Bulk pricing tiers (if applicable)
   - Seasonal pricing variations
   - Minimum advertised price (MAP) if required

3. Inventory Data:
   - Stock levels
   - Expected restock dates
   - Lead times
   - Warehouse locations
   - Shipping options

GOVERNANCE RULES
----------------
Automated governance enforces:

Pricing Rules:
- Retail price must be >= wholesale price
- Minimum margin: 5% (configurable)
- Maximum daily price change: 20%
- MAP enforcement (where applicable)
- Category allow/block lists

Category Rules:
- Proper categorization required
- Restricted categories need approval
- Multi-category products handled specially
- Seasonal reclassification allowed

Data Quality:
- SKU must be unique
- Product names required
- Valid pricing (positive numbers)
- Category must exist in system
- Availability status accurate

PRICING BEST PRACTICES
----------------------
1. Maintain Competitive Pricing:
   - Monitor market rates weekly
   - Adjust for supply/demand
   - Consider competitor actions
   - Factor in shipping costs

2. Seasonal Adjustments:
   - Plan ahead for high seasons
   - Clearance pricing for slow periods
   - Holiday promotions (advance notice)
   - Weather-dependent adjustments

3. Margin Management:
   - Maintain minimum 5% margin
   - Target 15-25% for most categories
   - Higher margins for specialty items
   - Consider total cost of ownership

4. Price Change Frequency:
   - Avoid excessive changes (daily limit enforced)
   - Batch related price updates
   - Provide advance notice for large changes
   - Document pricing rationale

CATALOG MANAGEMENT
------------------
Keep your catalog up to date:

1. Product Information:
   - Accurate descriptions
   - Complete specifications
   - High-quality images
   - Proper categorization
   - Current availability

2. Regular Updates:
   - Weekly inventory reviews
   - Monthly catalog audits
   - Quarterly pricing reviews
   - Annual product lifecycle review

3. New Product Introduction:
   - Submit 2 weeks in advance
   - Include all required fields
   - Provide images and specs
   - Set initial pricing strategically
   - Plan launch promotion

4. Discontinued Products:
   - Mark as inactive promptly
   - Clear inventory before removal
   - Archive historical data
   - Notify affected customers
